# Tidy Haven image shot list

Replace each current stock image with one licensed, consistent image in your brand style. The site uses six editorial images; the decorative logo/character elements are CSS/text and do not need image replacements.

## Recommended creative direction

- **Mood:** calm, warm, attainable, lived-in, quietly premium.
- **Light:** natural window light; soft shadows; warm-neutral color balance.
- **Styling:** functional rather than overly sterile. A few personal objects are welcome, but surfaces should look easy to use.
- **People:** optional. If included, show a real, relaxed moment—hands arranging a shelf, a family using a system—rather than posed stock-photo smiles.
- **Avoid:** extreme all-white minimalism, visibly fake "before/after" clutter, plastic-bin overload, heavy filters, cramped wide-angle distortion, and identifiable client documents/medication/address details.
- **Art direction:** favor sage green, soft clay, cream, oak, linen, and natural texture so imagery supports the existing site palette.

## Optional images for a stronger future version

These are not needed to replace existing images, but they would make strong additions when the site expands:

1. **Founder portrait:** Natural-light portrait in a client-ready outfit, ideally in a calm home environment. Conveys personal trust and makes a high-touch service feel more approachable.
2. **At-work detail:** Hands measuring a drawer, labeling bins, folding linens, or planning a shelf. Conveys expertise without requiring a client’s face.
3. **Move-in story:** A styled, organized new-home kitchen or closet with a few unopened boxes subtly in the background. Conveys the move-management offer.
4. **Before/after pair:** Same angle, professionally shot. Use restrained, believable contrast—not shame-based clutter. Conveys tangible transformation.
5. **Client lifestyle image:** A family or homeowner naturally enjoying a newly usable space. Conveys that the service changes daily life, not merely shelf appearance.
